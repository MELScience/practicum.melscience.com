Prepare an acid (i.e., HCl) solution of unknown concentration. 2. Pour this solution into a conical flask. Add a few drops of the phenolphthalein solution. 3. Fill a buret with a base (i.e., NaOH) solution of known concentration. 4. Add the base solution drop by drop into the flask. The solution becomes bright purple, when the equivalence point is obtained. safety_description: | Wear eye protection and lab gloves. Avoid contact of acid and base with skin. science_background: | The neutralization reaction between base and acid occurs in the solution. Concentration of hydrogen ions is changed - pH increases. Phenolphthalein presence in the acidic solution is in the colorless molecular form H2Ind. When the base is added, phenolphthalein turns to the ion form Ind2-.
