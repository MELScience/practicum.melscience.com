To make sand thermite prepare the following components: sand, aluminum powder and sulfur. 2. For better result the common sand should be treated with diluted hydrochloric acid to remove all the impurities. After treating rinse the sand with water. 3. Grind each substance in a mortar to get a finer powder. 4. Mix the components in the following ratio: 9 parts of sand, 12 parts of sulfur and 10 parts of aluminum. 5. Prepare a strong container (a clay flower pot for example). 6. Fill it by half with sand. 7. Pour the sand, sulfur and aluminum mixture into the pot in a hill-like shape. Make a socket on top of the hill. 8. Place a few grams of potassium permanganate into the socket and insert the magnesium strip. 9. Set the pot with the termite mixture outdoors in a safe place. Make sure that there are no flammable materials nearby. 10. Put the few drops of glycerin on top of the pill to cover the magnesium strip. 11. The mixture will burn in a few minutes with a bright blue flame and evolution of a large amount of heat. 12. Let the pot to cool down. 13. The large chunk of solid substance was formed in the thermite reaction. To obtain pure silicon break down the chunk and treat it with diluted hydrochloric acid. Then rinse it with water and dry. These operations also should be performed outside because the presence of sulfur compounds. safety_description: | Wear eye protection goggles and gloves. There is a serious fire risk - a fire extinguisher should be ready. Make sure there is nothing flammable nearby. science_background: | Thermite is a class of exothermic reduction-oxidation reactions. They are characterized by extremely high temperatures generated. As a rule termite composition consists of metal powder which plays a role of a fuel and metal oxide. In the described experiment elemental aluminum reduces the silica oxide (sand). In this reaction silica oxide behaves itself like a metal oxide besides the silicon properties lie in between metals and nonmetals. As a result the metallic silica and aluminum oxide are formed. It is not easy to make aluminum and silica oxide react since the activation energy of this reaction is very high. The energy needed to overcome the activation barrier can be supported by heat produced in another reaction. The side reaction of sulfur and aluminum is used for this purpose. These components react much more readily with evolution of heat enough to start the main reaction. The presence of sulfur causes the blue color of thermite flame and the special smell of the produced smoke. The initial ignition of the mixture is also made chemically. The reaction of potassium permanganate and glycerin produces fire without matches which ignites the magnesium ribbon which in turn runs the reaction between sulfur and aluminum.
