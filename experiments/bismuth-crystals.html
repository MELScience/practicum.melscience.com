Prepare two stainless steel measuring cups, a gas burner or a hot plate. 2. Put a piece of bismuth metal into the first cup. Impurities will affect the crystal growth so try to get as pure metal as possible. 3. Place the cup with bismuth on a hot plate. 4. Heat it until the metal melts. 5. Gray “skin” appears on the surface. It is formed by a large amount of bismuth oxide. 6. Heat the clean empty cup. 7. Slowly pour the liquid bismuth into the clean preheated cup. Try to avoid hitting the gray “skin”. 8. Turn off the hot plate. Place the cup with bismuth on it. 9. Crystals begin forming under cooling. 10. Before bismuth fully solidifies pour the excess of molten metal to another cup. 11. Crystals of very interesting shape are obtained. The crystal surface is iridescent. 12. If you don’t like the crystal shape you can remelt it and try again. safety_description: | Wear eye protection goggles and gloves. Try to avoid breathing in melting metal fumes. Use pliers and tweezers to handle hot objects. science_background: | Since bismuth has a relatively low melting point (271oC) it is possible to convert it into liquid state at home using a common gas burner or a hot plate. Under cooling it forms crystals of very peculiar shape resembling labyrinths with spirals and stairs. This is the result of different growth rate around the outside and inside edges. The two main factors that affect the process of crystal formation – the presence of impurities and the speed of melted metal cooling. The best crystals are obtained from pure metal under slow cooling. The color of Bismuth crystals also catches the eye. The oxide film is formed on the metal surface. The thickness of this film is different in different parts of the crystal and causes different wavelengths of light to interfere upon reflection.
