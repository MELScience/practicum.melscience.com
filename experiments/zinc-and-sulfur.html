Mix equal amounts of zinc powder and sulfur powder. 2. Put the mixture onto a nonflammable plate. 3. Ignite the mixture with a burning sliver or a burner. The mixture initially combusts weakly, then flashes and intensively burns. safety_description: | Wear lab gloves and eye protection. There is a serious fire risk - a fire extinguisher should be ready. Make sure there is nothing flammable nearby. There are many sparks in this experiment - use a protection shield! This experiment must be performed in a well-ventilated room or in a fume hood. science_background: | Zinc and sulfur react forming zinc sulfide. This reaction is very violent and produces a lot of heat, but ignition is needed to initiate it.
