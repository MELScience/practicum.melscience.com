Take 4 g of solid cobalt(II) chloride-6-water and dissolve it in 40 ml of water in a 250 ml heat resistant beaker. You have a light pink solution. 2. Put the beaker on a magnetic mixer, dip a stir bar into the solution and turn on the mixing and heating. 3. Add about 60 mL of concentrated hydrochloric acid to the beaker. You have a violet or blue solution. The color of the mixture depends on the ration between the acid and cobalt(II) chloride. 4. Add a small amount of water to the beaker to make the solution pink. 5. Add a small amount of hydrochloric acid to the beaker to make the solution blue. safety_description: | Wear eye protection goggles. Be careful with the concentrated acid! Don't drink or eat cobalt compounds and cobalt-containing solutions, they are very toxic! science_background: | The two different colored Co(II) complex ions, [Co(H2O)6]2+ and [CoCl4]2-, exist together in equilibrium in the solution in the presence of chloride ions: (Co(H2O)6)2+(aq)(pink) + 4Cl-(aq) <--> (CoCl4)2-(aq)(blue) + 6H2O safety: 7 coolness: 5 fire: 0 heating_with_fire: 0 explosion: 0 poisoned_gas: 0 heating_with_fire: 0 organic: 0 electricity: 0 solution: 1 oxydation_reduction: 0 color_change: 1 precipitate: 0 gassing: 0 catalyst: 0 phases_gas: 0 phases_liquide: 1 phases_solid: 1 ---
