Dissolved molecules easily pass into air from the concentrated solution. In the gas phase, molecules readily extend beyond the vessels. Ammonia and hydrogen chloride molecules react to form a new molecule called ammonium chloride. Separate molecules of ammonia chloride combine together to form a crystal. A small crystal nucleus grows bigger and bigger as new molecules of ammonia chloride join to it. Sometimes a couple of small crystals combine together giving a greater one. These new crystals can combine in their turn and so on. This process continues until particles become several thousandths of a millimeter in diameter.
