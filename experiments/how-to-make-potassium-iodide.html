Pour some water into a flask and dissolve some potassium hydroxide in it. 2. Put iodine crystals into the flask and heat it with a burner. All iodine should dissolve and react with potassium hydroxide. If the solution becomes brown there is an excess of iodine. In this case just add more potassium hydroxide. 3. Cool the flask down. Potassium iodate precipitates in the cold solution. 4. Filter potassium iodate. 5. This is the disproportionation reaction.
