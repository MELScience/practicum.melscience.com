Avoid contact of hydrogen peroxide with skin. science_background: | TCPO reacts with hydrogen peroxide and produces an intermediate - 1,2-dioxetanedione. It decomposes to CO2 and excites a fluorophore. So the chemical reaction energy transfers to light emission energy of the excited fluorophore in these reactions. The wavelength of the emitted light depends on the nature of the flurophore. Thus BPEA emits green light, rubrene - yellow, 9,10-diphenylanthracene - blue, rhodamine B - red. There is no light emission without a fluorophore in this reaction.
