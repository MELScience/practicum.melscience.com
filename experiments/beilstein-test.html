Make a spiral of copper wire. 2. Heat the spiral over an alcohol lamp until it’s red hot. 3. Wait until the wire cools. 4. Lower the wire in any halogenated organic compound. For example, you can take chloroform. 5. Put the wire into the flame again. 6. Observe the green flames. 7. Repeat steps 2-6 for the other compounds. Make sure that the test is positive for halogens (except fluorine). For example, you can also use 1-bromobutane and any product made from polyvinyl chloride. safety_description: | Wear eye protection goggles. Use gloves. science_background: | Heating copper in air leads to the formation of copper oxide. Copper oxide reacts with halogenated organics under heating and leads to the formation of copper salts, which make the flame green.
