A Handbook for Teachers of Chemistry, Volume 3) language: en step_by_step: | 1. Pour 50 ml of water into a small beaker. 2. Dissolve 7 grams of copper sulphate in water. 3. Add a small amount of hydrazine hydrate in the beaker. 4. Heat the beaker in a water bath for copper deposition on the walls. 5. Observe the formation of a smooth layer of metallic copper within 15 minutes. 6. The layer of copper can easily be washed off using a brush. safety_description: | Wear eye protection goggles. science_background: | Amongst the noble metals copper is most difficult to reduce. Hydrazine is one of the strongest reducing agents, due to the presence of nitrogen in a negative oxidation state in the molecule, which is soluble in water.
