Carefully put a drop of mercury into a watch glass. 2. Pour 6M sulfuric acid to just cover the mercury drop. 3. Add small amount of potassium dichromate. 4. Touch to mercury surface with an iron nail. The nail should be immersed in the sulfuric acid. The tip of the nail should just touch the mercury surface. The mercury drop begins to pulsate. safety_description: | Wear lab goggles and gloves. Mercury vapours are very toxic! This experiment must be performed in a fume hood. science_background: | Pulsation of the mercury drop is due a change of the surface tension. In the acid solution mercury is oxidized, and surface tension decreases. Mercury drop contacts with iron nail, and iron reduces the mercury ions to mercury metal because it has a sufficiently negative electrochemical potential. Surface tension of mercury increases, and the drop shrinks. Thus the contact of mercury and iron is broken and this oscillation cycle repeats. safety: 5 coolness: 5 fire: 0 heating_with_fire: 0 explosion: 0 poisoned_gas: 0 organic: 0 electricity: 0 solution: 0 oxydation_reduction: 1 color_change: 0 precipitate: 0 gassing: 0 catalyst: 0 phases_gas: 0 phases_liquide: 1 phases_solid: 0 ---
