Take a fire-resistance test tube. Fix the tube in a special holder or take the test tube with metal tongs. 2. Put a small amount of iron oxalate into the tube. 3. Evenly heat the tube with a gas burner. Heat the iron oxalate till it turns black. 4. Turn the test tube over and pour the iron on a refractory plate. You should carry this experiment out in a dark room for more efficiency. safety_description: | The test tube MUST be made of heat safe glass. Use tongs or a holder for holding the tube during heating. Be careful with the gas burner. There is a fire risk - a fire extinguisher should be ready. science_background: | A pyrophoric substance ignites spontaneously in air. Examples are iron sulfide and many reactive metals including uranium, when powdered or thinly sliced. In this experiment, finely divided iron has pyrophoric properties.
